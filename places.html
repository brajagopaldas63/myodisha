Daringbadi(Kashmir Of Odisha)


Daringbadi, often referred to as the "Kashmir of Odisha," is a picturesque hill station located in the Kandhamal district of Odisha, India. It is known for its scenic beauty, pleasant weather, and lush greenery, making it a popular destination for nature lovers and travelers.




Hirakud-dam(Smbalpur)


Hirakud is renowned for the Hirakud Dam, one of the longest dams in the world and a major engineering marvel in India. It is located on the Mahanadi River near Sambalpur in Odisha. The dam is not only a vital source of hydroelectric power and irrigation but also a significant tourist destination.




Chandipur(Invisible Sea Beach)


Chandipur, also known as Chandipur-on-sea, is a unique and serene beach town located in the Balasore district of Odisha, India. It is famous for its receding waters, as the sea retreats up to 5 kilometers during low tide, creating a mesmerizing and unique natural phenomenon.




Jeypore Waterfalls and Caves


Also known as the Matsya Tirtha, this waterfall is one of the highest in India, with a height of 157 meters. Formed by the Machkund River, it lies on the Odisha-Andhra Pradesh border and is a major attraction due to its breathtaking cascade and surrounding greenery. The area is also significant for hydroelectric power generation.




Similipal National Park


Similipal National Park, located in the Mayurbhanj district of Odisha, is one of Indiaâ€™s largest national parks and a UNESCO World Network of Biosphere Reserves. Spread across approximately 2,750 square kilometers, the park is named after the "Simul" (silk cotton) trees that bloom abundantly in the region. It is famous for its rich biodiversity, scenic beauty, waterfalls, and tribal culture.




Bhitarkanika National Park("Mini Amazon of India")


Bhitarkanika National Park, located in the Kendrapara district of Odisha, is a breathtaking ecosystem of mangroves, estuaries, and deltaic regions formed by the Brahmani, Baitarani, and Dhamra rivers. Renowned for its lush greenery, rich biodiversity, and serene waterways, it is often referred to as the "Mini Amazon of India".




Satkosia Tiger Reserve


Satkosia Tiger Reserve, located in the Angul, Cuttack, Nayagarh, and Boudh districts of Odisha, is a spectacular haven of biodiversity and natural beauty. Established in 1976, it spans across 964 square kilometers and is named after the Satkosia Gorge, a magnificent 22-kilometer-long canyon carved by the Mahanadi River.




Chilika Lake and Kalijai Temple


Chilika Lake, located in Odisha, is the largest brackish water lagoon in Asia and a UNESCO-recognized Ramsar Wetland of International Importance. It spans over 1,100 square kilometers, encompassing Puri, Khurda, and Ganjam districts. The lake is renowned for its rich biodiversity, stunning natural beauty, and cultural significance, with the Kalijai Temple being a prominent spiritual landmark.




Bhimkund


Bhimkund, located in the Mayurbhanj district of Odisha, is a mesmerizing natural gorge and a sacred pilgrimage site associated with Hindu mythology. The site features a deep water tank surrounded by breathtaking rocky landscapes and lush greenery, making it both a spiritual and natural marvel.




Devkund


Bhimkund, located in the Mayurbhanj district of Odisha, is a mesmerizing natural gorge and a sacred pilgrimage site associated with Hindu mythology. The site features a deep water tank surrounded by breathtaking rocky landscapes and lush greenery, making it both a spiritual and natural marvel.




Deomali


Deomali, located in the Koraput district of Odisha, is the highest peak in the state, standing at an elevation of 1,672 meters (5,489 feet) above sea level. Nestled in the Eastern Ghats, Deomali offers stunning panoramic views, cool weather, and a tranquil environment, making it a favorite destination for nature lovers, trekkers, and adventure enthusiasts.




Panchalingeswar


Panchalingeswar, located in the Balasore district of Odisha, is a serene hilltop destination famous for its natural beauty and the ancient Panchalingeswar Temple. Nestled in the Nilagiri Hills, this spot is a blend of spirituality and nature, making it a popular attraction for pilgrims and nature lovers alike




Chandaka Wildlife Sanctuar


Chandaka Wildlife Sanctuary, located near Bhubaneswar, Odisha, is a haven for nature enthusiasts and wildlife lovers. Covering an area of approximately 193 square kilometers, it is nestled in the Khurda district and is part of the larger Chilika Lake Ecosystem, with lush forests and diverse wildlife. The sanctuary is particularly known for its rich flora, fauna, and scenic landscapes.
