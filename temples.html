Puri-Jagannath Temple


Location: Puri
Dedicated to : Lord Jagannath, an incarnation of Lord Vishnu
Significance: One of the Char Dham pilgrimage sites and famous for the annual Rath Yatra.
Unique Feature: The temple's flag always flutters in the opposite direction of the wind.



Konark Sun-Temple


Location: Konark
        Dedicated to: Sun God (Surya)
        Significance: A UNESCO World Heritage Site; built in the shape of a massive chariot.
        Unique Feature: Intricate carvings and its alignment with the sun's rays.



Lingaraj Temple


Location: Bhubaneswar
        Dedicated to: Lord Shiva
        Significance: One of the largest temples in Odisha and a classic example of Kalinga architecture.
        Unique Feature: A blend of Shaivism and Vaishnavism.



Emami Jagannath Temple


Location: Balasore
        Established By: Emami Group, a leading business conglomerate,
    with a focus on creating a spiritual and architectural masterpiece.
   Dedicated To: Lord Jagannath, along with his siblings Lord Balabhadra and 
   Goddess Subhadra.



Chandaneswar Shiv Temple


Location: Balasore
        The Chaitra Mela or Chaitra Parva is the most famous festival celebrated here.
Held in the Hindu month of Chaitra, it attracts hundreds of thousands of devotees who visit the temple to offer prayers and perform rituals.
Special ceremonies and cultural events are organized during the festival.



Mukteshwar Temple


Location: Bhubaneswar
        Dedicated to: Lord Shiva
Significance: Known as the "Gem of Odisha Architecture."
Unique Feature: The intricate carvings and its unique torana (arched gateway).



Rajarani Temple


Location: Bhubaneswar
        Dedicated to: No deity (non-functional temple)
Significance: Famous for its ornate carvings.
Unique Feature: Made from a beautiful reddish-gold sandstone called "Rajarani."



Brahmeswara Temple


Location: Bhubaneswar
        Dedicated to: Lord Shiva
Significance: Known for its architectural finesse.
Unique Feature: Richly decorated interiors and exteriors.



Taratarini Temple


Location: Ganjam
  Dedicated to: Tara and Tarini, forms of Goddess Shakti
Significance: A Shakti Peetha, significant for Tantric practices.
Unique Feature: Located on a hilltop with a scenic view.



Ananta Vasudeva Temple


Location: Bhubaneswar
    Dedicated to: Lord Krishna
Significance: A Vaishnavite temple in a city dominated by Shaivite shrines.
Unique Feature: Temple architecture is similar to the Lingaraj Temple.



Cuttack Chandi Temple


Location: Cuttack
        Dedicated to: Goddess Chandi, an incarnation of Durga
        Significance: Revered as the presiding deity of Cuttack.
        Unique Feature: A center of Durga Puja celebrations in Odisha



Maa Samaleswari Temple


Location: Sambalpur
        Dedicated to: Goddess Samaleswari, the presiding deity of Sambalpur
        Significance: Famous among Shakti worshippers.
        Unique Feature: Celebrated for the Nuakhai festival.



Bhusundeswar Temple


Location: Balasore
        Baba Bhusandeswar Temple, is one of the largest Shiva Lingams 
        in the World is in Bhogarai village of Balasore district, Odisha.
        It ia said, that the shiv linga situated here, is naturally inclined
        in position and the largest in asia.
