Ratha Yatra (Car Festival)


Celebrated in Puri, it is one of Odisha's most famous festivals. Dedicated to Lord Jagannath, it involves pulling massive chariots of Lord Jagannath, Balabhadra, and Subhadra. Held annually in June or July.




Durga Puja


A grand celebration across Odisha, especially in cities like Cuttack, Bhubaneswar,Rourkela,Baleshwar. Known for elaborate pandals and traditional rituals.




Makar Sankranti


Known locally as Makara Sankranti, this festival marks the harvest season. People prepare special dishes like Makara Chaula (uncooked rice with jaggery and fruits).




Pana Sankranti (Odia New Year)


Celebrated in April, it marks the Odia New Year. Devotees prepare a traditional drink called Pana made of jaggery, water, and fruit.




Diwali


Diwali, also known as the Festival of Lights, is widely celebrated in Odisha, but with some unique cultural and traditional variations compared to other parts of India. Here's how Diwali is observed in the state




Bali Jatra


Celebrated in Cuttack to commemorate Odishaâ€™s ancient maritime trade. Includes a massive fair with cultural programs, food stalls, and handicrafts.




Raja Parba


A unique three-day festival dedicated to womanhood and fertility. Celebrates the onset of the monsoon and agricultural season.




Chandan Yatra


Celebrated in Puri, dedicated to Lord Jagannath. Deities are taken on a boat ride, symbolizing their leisure.




Dola Purnima (Holi)


Celebrated with colors, similar to Holi, but with an added emphasis on the worship of Lord Krishna. Idols are carried on palanquins in processions.




Kumar Purnima


Celebrated by young unmarried girls who worship the moon and pray for a good match. Includes singing, dancing, and traditional rituals.




Magha Saptami (Chandrabhaga Mela)


Celebrated at the Chandrabhaga Beach, near Konark, in honor of the Sun God. Devotees take a holy dip in the sea at dawn.




Nuakhai


A harvest festival, particularly popular in Western Odisha. Families celebrate with a grand feast after offering the first harvest to the deities.




Kartika Purnima


Celebrated by floating small boats made of banana bark, known as Boita Bandana, in water bodies. Commemorates Odisha's ancient maritime history.




Ganesh Chaturthi


Celebrated with devotion in many parts of Odisha, honoring Lord Ganesha.
