Pakhala(It's not only food it's our emotion)


Pakhala is a traditional and beloved dish from Odisha, India, often consumed during the summer months due to its cooling properties. It is a type of fermented rice dish, made by soaking cooked rice in water and leaving it to ferment overnight. The dish is typically served with a variety of accompaniments to enhance its flavor.








Abhada(Prabhu Jagannath Prasad)


Abhada is a traditional offering and meal served in temples, particularly in Odisha, as part of religious rituals. It is considered a sacred food prepared for the deity and later distributed to the devotees as prasad (a blessed offering). The process of making and serving Abhada is deeply rooted in the cultural and religious practices of Odisha.








Chhena Poda


Chhena Poda is a famous traditional sweet dish from Odisha, often referred to as the "Cheese Cake of Odisha." It is made primarily from chhena (fresh paneer or Indian cottage cheese), and it holds a special place in Odia cuisine. The name "Chhena Poda" translates to "baked cheese," which reflects its preparation method.








Rasabali


Rasabali is a traditional and delicious sweet dish from Odisha, typically made with chhena (fresh paneer) and flavored with cardamom and saffron. It is a popular offering in temples, especially during festivals like Rath Yatra in Puri, where it is served as prasad (holy offering) to Lord Jagannath.

Rasgulla (also known as Rasgola in Odisha)


Rasgulla (also known as Rasgola in Odisha) is one of the most iconic and beloved sweets in India, particularly famous in the state of Odisha and West Bengal. It is made from chhena (fresh paneer or Indian cottage cheese) and sugar syrup, known for its spongy, soft, and syrup-soaked texture.








Enduri Pitha


Enduri Pitha is a traditional Odia sweet, especially popular during the Prathamastami and Kumar Purnima festivals, as well as in other seasonal celebrations. It is a type of steamed cake made from rice flour and filled with a delicious mixture of coconut, jaggery, and dal (lentils). The preparation is somewhat similar to other Odia pithas, but its unique flavor and steamed texture make it distinct.








Khichdi


Khichdi is a simple and comforting dish that holds a special place in Indian cuisine, including Odia cuisine. It is a nutritious, one-pot meal typically made with rice and lentils (usually moong dal or yellow split peas). Khichdi is considered light on the stomach and is often consumed during periods of fasting or illness due to its ease of digestion








Kanika


Kanika is a traditional and aromatic sweet rice dish from Odisha. It is often served as a prasad (holy offering) in temples, especially during festivals like Rath Yatra in Puri and other special occasions. Kanika is a fragrant, mildly sweet rice dish made with basmati rice, ghee, sugar, and a variety of spices and dry fruits.








Dahibara


Dahibara is a popular and beloved traditional dish from Odisha, often served as a street food or snack. It consists of fried vada-like dumplings made from urad dal (black gram) that are soaked in yogurt (dahi) and topped with tamarind chutney, spices, and sometimes onions or coriander leaves. It is a savory, tangy, and spicy dish that is refreshing and satisfying, commonly enjoyed as an evening snack or as part of a festive meal.








Machha Besara


Machha Besara is a classic and flavorful fish curry from Odisha, which is part of its rich and diverse seafood cuisine. The dish is known for its distinctive mustard-based gravy that is both tangy and spicy. "Machha" means fish in Odia, and "Besara" refers to a mustard-based curry. It is a comforting, aromatic dish commonly made with freshwater fish like rohu or hilsa and is often served with steamed rice.








Raabidi


Raabidi made with milk is a variation of the traditional sweet rice pudding in Odisha, where milk is the primary ingredient, making it creamy and rich. This version of Raabidi is a comforting, delicious dessert typically prepared during festivals or special occasions in Odia households. It is different from regular rice pudding, as it uses jaggery as the sweetener, and is often flavored with cardamom, coconut, and dry fruits.








Lassi


Lassi is a traditional yogurt-based drink popular in India and Pakistan, known for its refreshing taste and cooling properties. It is often consumed during the hot summer months or alongside heavy meals to aid digestion. Lassi can be enjoyed in both sweet and salty variations, each offering a distinct flavor profile. In Odia cuisine, lassi is typically made in its sweet form, although salty lassi is also consumed in various regions of India.








Aludam


In Odisha, Aludam is commonly served during festivals like Nuakhai, Makar Sankranti, and other special family occasions. It is also a staple in many Odia households as a quick, flavorful dish that is both satisfying and easy to prepare. The combination of spices, tanginess, and the comfort of potatoes makes it a beloved dish across the state.








Mudi


Mudi is a traditional snack or street food commonly found in Odisha and several other parts of Eastern India. It is made from puffed rice (locally called muri in Odisha), and is often mixed with a variety of spices, vegetables, and herbs to create a savory snack. Mudi is typically light and crunchy, making it a popular choice as a light meal or snack, especially during festivals, gatherings, or casual street food settings.
